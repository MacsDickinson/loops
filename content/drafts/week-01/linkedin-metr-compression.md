## Draft

METR's latest AI task completion research shows something striking:

Tasks that took experienced engineers 2-4 hours in 2023 now take AI agents 15-30 minutes.

That's not incremental. That's a phase change.

But here's what the data doesn't show:

When build time compresses 10x, the bottleneck doesn't disappear. It migrates.

What I'm seeing in practice:

Teams adopt AI coding tools → productivity spikes for 2-3 sprints → then plateaus.

Why? Because the new bottleneck isn't build. It's requirements clarity.

You can generate code in 15 minutes. But if the spec is ambiguous, you just get wrong code in 15 minutes.

The shift:
- Cost of being wrong → drops dramatically (cheap to rebuild)
- Cost of being unclear → stays the same (expensive to discover post-build)

The teams sustaining velocity gains aren't the ones with the best AI prompts.

They're the ones who already had tight discovery loops. Clear acceptance criteria. Structured product conversations.

AI made the build fast. But it exposed everything upstream that was already slow.
