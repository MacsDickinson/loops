## Draft

Every innovation in software delivery has been about the same thing:

Tightening feedback loops.

Agile → shorter cycles between plan and release
DevOps → faster loop from code to production
Lean → tighter loop from idea to validation
XP → "if feedback is good, more feedback is better"

AI isn't different. It's just the latest loop-tightening technology.

WHERE the loop tightens is what changes.
the underlying principle's dont - that fast feedback beats slow feedback.

Here's what I'm seeing play out:

The teams struggling with AI coding tools are trying to bolt AI onto waterfall processes.

The teams winning? They already thought in feedback loops. AI just makes them faster.

If your delivery process didn't work before AI, making it faster won't fix it.

The constraint just moves somewhere else.

What delivery practices did you already have in place that made AI adoption smoother?
